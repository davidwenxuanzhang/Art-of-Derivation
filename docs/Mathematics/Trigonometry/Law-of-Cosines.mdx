  <div>
   For a random $\triangle ABC$, $a$, $b$, and $c$ are the length of the sides of the triangle, and seperately, $\angle A$, $\angle B$, and $\angle C$ are the opposite angles, then the Law of Cosines points out:

$$
    c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cos{\angle C}  
$$
And similarly: 
$$
    a^2=b^2+c^2-2bc\cos{\angle A}
$$

$$
    b^2=a^2+c^2-2ac\cos{\angle B}
$$
