Heron's Formula

Heron's Theorem

Given a △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC with the side length aaa, bbb, and ccc, then, the area is:

S△ABC=p(p−a)(p−b)(p−c) S_{\triangle{ABC}}=\sqrt{p(p-a)(p-b)(p-c)}S△ABC​=p(p−a)(p−b)(p−c)​

where p=a+b+c2p=\frac{a+b+c}{2}p=2a+b+c​, as half of the semiperimeter of △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC

Qin Jiushao's formula

S△ABC=14[a2c2−(a2−b2+c22)2] S_{\triangle{ABC}}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}\left[a^2c^2-\left(\frac{a^2-b^2+c^2}{2}\right)^2 \right]}S△ABC​=41​[a2c2−(2a2−b2+c2​)2]​
