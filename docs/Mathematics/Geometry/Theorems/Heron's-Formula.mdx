# Heron's Formula

:::tip Heron's Theorem

<div className="deriv2col">

  <div>
    <img src={useBaseUrl('/img/Heron-Formula.png')} width="375" height="322" />
  </div>

  <div>
    Given a $\triangle ABC$ with the side length $a$, $b$, and $c$, then, the area is: 


$$
   S_{\triangle{ABC}}=\sqrt{p(p-a)(p-b)(p-c)}
$$


    where $p=\frac{a+b+c}{2}$, as half of the semiperimeter of $\triangle ABC$ 

<Admonition type="note" title="Qin Jiushao's formula">
  Alternatively, here is an equivalent form of Heron's formula
$$
   S_{\triangle{ABC}}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}\left[a^2c^2-\left(\frac{a^2-b^2+c^2}{2}\right)^2 \right]}
$$
</Admonition>
  </div>
</div>
<br/>
<details>
<summary><span className="math-keyword">Derivation</span></summary>

<div className="deriv2col" style={{ gridTemplateColumns: "300px 1fr", gap: "1.5rem" }}>

  <div>
    <img src={useBaseUrl('/img/Heron-Formula-Derivation.png')} width="320" height="230" />
  </div>

  <div>

Let $BH \perp AC$; $H$ is a point on $AC$. 

From the [Pythagorean Theorem](docs\Mathematics\Geometry\Theorems\Pythagorean-Theorem.mdx), we get: 

$$
\begin{align*}
    a^2-x^2&=b^2-(c-x)^2\\
    a^2-x^2&=b^2-c^2+2cx-x^2\\
    2cx&=a^2-b^2+c^2\\
    x&=\frac{a^2-b^2+c^2}{2c}
\end{align*}
$$
  </div>
</div>
$$
  \begin{align*}
      S_{\triangle{ABC}}&=\frac{1}{2}AC\cdot BH \\
                        &=\frac{1}{2}a\cdot \sqrt{c^2-\left(\frac{a^2-b^2+c^2}{2c}\right)^2}\\
                        &=\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}\left[a^2c^2-\left(\frac{a^2-b^2+c^2}{2}\right)^2 \right]} &&\text{(Qin Jiushao's formula)}\\
                        &=\sqrt{\frac{1}{16}[4a^2c^2-(a^2-b^2+c^2)^2]}\\
                        &=\sqrt{\frac{(2ac)^2-(a^2-b^2+c^2)^2}{16}}\\
                        &=\sqrt{\frac{(2ac+a^2-b^2+c^2)(2ac-a^2+b^2-c^2)}{16}}\\
                        &=\sqrt{\frac{[(a+c)^2-b^2][b^2-(a-c)^2]}{16}}\\
                        &=\sqrt{\frac{(a+c+b)(a+c-b)(b+a-c)(b-a+c)}{16}}\\
                        &=\sqrt{\frac{a+b+c}{2}\frac{-a+b+c}{2}\frac{a-b+c}{2}\frac{a+b-c}{2}}\\
                        &=\sqrt{\frac{a+b+c}{2}\left(\frac{a+b+c}{2}-a\right)\left(\frac{a+b+c}{2}-b\right)\left(\frac{a+b+c}{2}-c\right)}\\
  \end{align*}
$$
Now, let $p=\frac{a+b+c}{2}$, then: 

$$
   S_{\triangle{ABC}}=\sqrt{p(p-a)(p-b)(p-c)} \tag*{$\blacksquare$}
$$

</details>
