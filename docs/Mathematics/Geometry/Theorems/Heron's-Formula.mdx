# Heron's Formula

:::tip Heron's Theorem

<div className="deriv2col">

  <div>
    <img src={useBaseUrl('/img/Heron-Formula.png')} width="375" height="320" />
  </div>

  <div>
    Given a $\triangle ABC$ with the side length $a$, $b$, and $c$, then, the area is: 


$$
   S_{\triangle{ABC}}=\sqrt{p(p-a)(p-b)(p-c)}
$$


    where $p=\frac{a+b+c}{2}$, as half of the semiperimeter of $\triangle ABC$ 

<Admonition type="note" title="Qin Jiushao's formula">
  Alternatively, here is an equivalent form of Heron's formula
$$
   S_{\triangle{ABC}}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}\left[a^2b^2-\left(\frac{a^2+b^2-c^2}{2}\right)^2 \right]}
$$
</Admonition>
  </div>

</div>
