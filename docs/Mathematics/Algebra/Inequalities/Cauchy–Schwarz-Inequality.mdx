:::tip Cauchy–Schwarz Inequality

<Admonition type="note" title="Real Number Case">
The following version of the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality is for real numbers only; more general forms exist in complex and vector settings.
</Admonition>

Given two finite number sequence in the real number $\{x_1,x_2,\cdots,x_n\}$ and $\{y_1,y_2,\cdots,y_n\}$, the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality states: 

$$
\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_iy_i \right)^2\leq \left(\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_i^2 \right)\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n}y_i^2 \right)
$$
in which 
$$
(x_1y_1+x_2y_2+\cdots +x_n y_n)^2\leq (x_1^2+x_2^2+\cdots+x_n^2)(y_1^2+y_2^2+\cdots+y_n^2)
$$

This inequality equal when there exists a real number $\lambda$ such that $x_i=\lambda b_i$
:::
